Research Methodology

My research employs a multidisciplinary approach combining theoretical analysis, simulation modeling, and real-world validation.

1

Problem Identification & Literature Review

Identifying critical transportation challenges through stakeholder engagement and comprehensive literature analysis to understand current state-of-the-art and research gaps.

Systematic Reviews Stakeholder Interviews Gap Analysis
2

Theoretical Framework Development

Developing mathematical models and theoretical frameworks that capture the essence of transportation phenomena and form the foundation for innovative solutions.

Mathematical Modeling Game Theory Optimization Theory Statistical Analysis
3

Simulation & Modeling

Creating comprehensive simulation environments to test and validate theoretical concepts, allowing for controlled experimentation and scenario analysis.

SUMO VISSIM Python/MATLAB Custom Simulators
4

Real-World Validation

Validating simulation results through field studies, data collection, and pilot implementations to ensure practical applicability and effectiveness.

Field Studies Data Collection Pilot Testing Performance Metrics
5

Implementation & Technology Transfer

Translating research outcomes into practical applications through industry partnerships, technology transfer, and policy recommendations.

Industry Collaboration Patent Filing Policy Development Commercialization

Current Research Highlights

Ongoing research initiatives that are pushing the boundaries of transportation engineering and connected vehicle technologies.

Ongoing

AI-Powered Traffic Signal Optimization

Developing machine learning algorithms that adapt traffic signal timing in real-time based on connected vehicle data and traffic patterns.

Expected Impact: 25-30% reduction in urban traffic delays
Collaborators: Ford Motor Company, City of Mumbai
Ongoing

V2X Communication in Adverse Weather

Investigating how connected vehicle communication systems perform under various weather conditions and developing adaptive protocols.

Expected Impact: Enhanced safety in challenging weather conditions
Collaborators: Northwestern University, Leidos Corporation
Planning

Autonomous Vehicle Integration in Mixed Traffic

Studying the integration of autonomous vehicles in mixed traffic environments typical of Indian road conditions.

Expected Impact: Smoother transition to autonomous vehicle adoption
Collaborators: IIT Bombay SeDriCa Team, Industry Partners
